FAQ

  • It starts off pretty easy and slowly teaches you how everything works. New tools and objectives appear as you progress. Players quickly understand the core gameplay. You just kind of figure out the strategy as you play more. It’s easy to start but rewarding to master.

  • You can play the game without Wi-Fi or an internet connection. This makes it perfect for travel or relaxing breaks. Progress is saved automatically. Whether you are at home or on a train, the puzzles remain available. The clerk never misses a workday.

  • Boosters are tools that make difficult levels easier to solve. You can usually figure out a way to beat levels without using power-ups if you're clever about it. However, well-timed boosters can clear tricky situations. I usually hoard mine until I'm really stuck on a tough level. You'll definitely need to think more about when to use your power-ups as things get trickier.

  • As players advance, the boards introduce new layouts and obstacles. Token goals become more complex. Players must plan moves more carefully. Yeah, it gets tougher, but not in that hair-pulling way some games do. More like a nice steady climb that keeps you hooked.

  • Pretty much anyone can pick this up and play - my 8-year-old nephew loves it, and so does my mom. You just tap stuff - nothing complicated about it. Bright tokens and friendly animations make it engaging for younger players. Adults often enjoy the strategy and relaxing atmosphere. Families can enjoy playing together.

  • The ledger theme transforms match-3 gameplay into something more meaningful. Instead of just blowing stuff up like most puzzle games, you're actually filing things away, which is oddly therapeutic. That little blue guy is actually pretty cute - he gets all excited when you make good moves. Honestly, it's kinda weird how relaxing this is. Like, who knew organizing a fake desk could be so satisfying?
