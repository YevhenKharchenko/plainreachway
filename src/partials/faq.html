FAQ

  • Is this a fast-paced puzzle game?

    No. It's not one of those twitch games - you actually need to sit and think about your moves. You'll want to take a good look at the map before you start dropping towers everywhere. No rush at all - you can sit there and think about it as long as you want. It's all about planning, not speed.

  • How does signal range work?

    Each tower broadcasts a circular signal radius. For a message to travel across the map, every tower must fall within the signal range of the next tower in the chain. If there is a gap between ranges, the message cannot continue.

  • Can levels have multiple solutions?

    Yes. Many puzzles can be solved in different ways. But if you're trying to get a good score, you'll want to use as few towers as possible. If you're into that perfectionist thing, you'll probably end up going back to earlier levels trying to beat your tower count.

  • What happens when terrain blocks signals?

    Terrain features such as hills or interference zones can reduce or distort signal range. You gotta be really careful about where you put your towers to work around all that stuff. Sometimes the best route is not the most direct one.

  • Is the game suitable for short sessions?

    Absolutely. Most puzzles can be completed in a few minutes. Great for when you've got a few minutes to kill, but honestly you can end up playing for hours without realizing it.

  • Does the difficulty increase gradually?

    Yes. Early levels introduce the basic mechanics of tower placement and signal overlap. Later stages expand the map and introduce terrain complications and tighter tower limits. Each level builds on the last without throwing you in the deep end.
