So you've got this huge empty field, and somehow you need to get your signal from one end to the other.

So there's this huge, quiet landscape, and you've got a message that needs to get across it somehow.
Between the starting beacon and the distant receiver lies only open terrain.
Basically, you're building these tower chains to carry messages across the field. Each tower must connect with the next, forming a stable communication path.

The land is wide.
Your network must be precise.

What is this game?

It's this puzzle game where you're setting up communication networks across big open areas.
You place towers so their signal ranges overlap, allowing messages to travel from one point to another. You can't just plop towers anywhere - the terrain's tricky and you don't have unlimited towers to work with.
Here's the thing though - you can't just spam towers everywhere. You've gotta be smart about it, use as few as possible while keeping everything connected.

CORE MECHANICS

You basically just place towers, connect them up, and watch the signal go through.

  • Place signal towers across the landscape

  • Ensure signal ranges overlap between towers

  • Connect the starting beacon with the destination receiver

  • Solve each level using limited tower placements

  • Get everything hooked up right and the signals just do their thing.

SIGNAL NETWORKS

It's all about getting the range right.

Every tower broadcasts a signal across a circular range. For a message to travel successfully, each tower must fall within the range of the next.
If you mess up the placement, you'll get gaps in your network. Sure, you could just throw towers everywhere, but the game actually rewards you for being efficient.
The best setups are the ones that work without throwing towers all over the place.

TERRAIN CHALLENGES

Sometimes the map's just being a pain and nothing wants to line up right.

Later levels introduce terrain features that affect signal transmission.

  • hills that weaken signals

  • valleys that distort range

  • obstacles that block tower placement

  • environmental interference zones

So you'll have to get creative and figure out how to route around all this stuff that gets in your way.

LEVEL PROGRESSION

From simple connections to complex networks.

Early levels focus on understanding signal range and placement.

  • larger landscapes

  • stricter tower limits

  • complex terrain interference

  • multi-path routing challenges

After a while you stop just throwing towers around and actually start thinking about where to put them.

FAQ

  • Is this a fast-paced puzzle game?

    No. It's not one of those twitch games - you actually need to sit and think about your moves. You'll want to take a good look at the map before you start dropping towers everywhere. No rush at all - you can sit there and think about it as long as you want. It's all about planning, not speed.

  • How does signal range work?

    Each tower broadcasts a circular signal radius. For a message to travel across the map, every tower must fall within the signal range of the next tower in the chain. If there is a gap between ranges, the message cannot continue.

  • Can levels have multiple solutions?

    Yes. Many puzzles can be solved in different ways. But if you're trying to get a good score, you'll want to use as few towers as possible. If you're into that perfectionist thing, you'll probably end up going back to earlier levels trying to beat your tower count.

  • What happens when terrain blocks signals?

    Terrain features such as hills or interference zones can reduce or distort signal range. You gotta be really careful about where you put your towers to work around all that stuff. Sometimes the best route is not the most direct one.

  • Is the game suitable for short sessions?

    Absolutely. Most puzzles can be completed in a few minutes. Great for when you've got a few minutes to kill, but honestly you can end up playing for hours without realizing it.

  • Does the difficulty increase gradually?

    Yes. Early levels introduce the basic mechanics of tower placement and signal overlap. Later stages expand the map and introduce terrain complications and tighter tower limits. Each level builds on the last without throwing you in the deep end.
